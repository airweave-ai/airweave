Very good question! Making an argument under Original Contributions of Major Significance requires that we show not only that you have invented something new ("original contributions") but also that what you invented had major significance. There are typically two ways to show that an invention had major significance:
  • Public information if there is publicly available citation information (usually want to see 100+), or press about the invention. Let us know if this would be available - our understanding is that it probably isn't
  • Private documents from a company showing that the invention had major commercial or technical impact at the company. This usually looks like showing internal strategy documents linking one piece of work to commercial impact, or to important strategic initiatives. This is usually very difficult to obtain at big companies

In any case adding the extra Original Contributions argument from ASML wouldn't likely move the needle on the case that much since you already have strong Original Contributions at Airweave. The goal would be to get you one more criteria that you don't already meet that would be strong.
